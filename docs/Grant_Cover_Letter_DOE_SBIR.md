I am submitting a proposal for Phase I SBIR funding to develop and validate the Blackbody Solar Thermal Tower (BSTT), a revolutionary passive energy system that generates 131.8 kW of mechanical power from a single 100-foot structure with zero fuel consumption.

The Problem:
Remote communities and mining operations rely on expensive, polluting diesel generators or intermittent solar PV with costly battery storage.

The Solution:
Our technology utilizes a proprietary Aerocement matrix. This material acts as a 98% efficient solar blackbody absorber while maintaining a porous, capillary structure for passive evaporative cooling.
- Mechanism: The Aerocement panels form a 100-foot solar chimney. Solar heating creates a massive stack effect, driving air at high velocity through a Stirling engine.
- Dual Output: The system simultaneously generates mechanical power and 35F cooling.

Validation:
Thermodynamic simulations confirm that a 100-foot BSTT tower in a 110F environment can achieve:
- Exit Air Temperature: 400F.
- Mechanical Power Output: 131.8 kW.
- Efficiency: 15-20%.

Phase I Objectives:
With $275,000 in funding, we will:
1. Fabricate a 1:10 scale prototype (10ft tower) using Aerocement.
2. Conduct field testing to validate the Aerocement material properties.
3. Refine the Stirling engine coupling design.

Impact:
This project supports the DOE's mission to advance clean energy independence by providing a scalable, zero-fuel solution for off-grid locations.
