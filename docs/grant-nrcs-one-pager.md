## Conservation Challenge (SE Missouri Working Lands)
Delta-region farms face soil structure decline, pollinator habitat loss, inefficient water/nutrient cycling, high fossil-fuel costs for irrigation/processing/cold storage, and post-harvest spoilage that erodes viability. Extreme weather increases risk. Conventional conservation practices often lack integrated on-farm energy and processing components that make adoption profitable for mid-size and limited-resource producers.

## Integrated Solution (H-003 Thermal Cascade + AE-GFRC + Agroforestry)
Replicable pilot node on working land:
- **H-003 Passive Thermal Loop**: Volumetric open-cell aerocement panels (98% solar absorption) + solid-fill wet labyrinth at depth + separate hot (\~77°C) / cold (\~35°F) batteries deliver grid-free heat, cold, and mechanical work (Stirling belt/flywheel + TEG). Powers freeze-drying, dehydration, and climate control with zero fuel cost. Functional service multiplier >200% (replaces multiple grid services via passive buoyancy + latent exchange). All theoretical until Node Zero instrumentation.
- **Black Locust Agroforestry & Food Forest**: Nitrogen-fixing coppice backbone establishes pollinator habitat, improves soil aggregation/erosion control, supports biodiversity, and enables closed-loop aquaponics (tilapia + duckweed + spirulina + red wiggler) with high water/nutrient efficiency and minimal external inputs.
- **AE-GFRC Infrastructure**: Alkali-resistant glass-fiber reinforced aerocement for low-cost geodesic processing/storage/shelter structures (cardboard + acetone/silicone membrane base).
- **Resilience Layer**: Excess production freeze-dried for local food pantries and disaster response; open data accelerates replication.

Designs permanently open (GitHub + Zenodo + IPFS + Appropedia integration). No patents. Full producer replicability.
