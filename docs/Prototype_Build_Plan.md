# Prototype Build Plan (The "MVP")
Goal: Build a functional 8ft x 4ft x 1ft test column to validate the "Blackbody" absorption and "Capillary" cooling.
Budget: ~$300 - $500 (Materials only).
Timeline: 2 Weeks.

Phase A: Material Formulation (The "Aerocement")
Ingredients:
- Portland Cement: 1 bag (94 lbs).
- Fine Sand: 2 bags (50 lbs each).
- Activated Carbon Powder: 5 lbs (Aquarium stores or online).
- Foaming Agent: 1 cup Dawn Ultra OR commercial concrete foam.
- Xanthan Gum: 15g (Thickener).
- Isopropyl Alcohol: 200ml.
- Water: As needed.

Mix Ratio:
1 part Cement : 2 parts Sand : 0.1 parts Carbon : 0.5 parts Water + Foaming Agent.

Procedure:
1. Mix dry ingredients (Cement, Sand, Carbon) thoroughly.
2. Mix Xanthan Gum, Alcohol, and Dawn into water to create the gel.
3. Add gel to dry mix slowly while mixing.
4. Pour into a 4x8x1ft wooden mold.
5. Cure: Keep wet for 7 days.

Phase B: The Chimney Structure
- Frame: Build a simple wooden or PVC frame to hold the panel vertically.
- Insulation: Wrap the back of the panel with rigid foam insulation (R-10).
- Air Channel: Create a 1ft deep gap behind the panel using spacers.
- Intake/Exhaust: Cut 4-inch holes at the bottom (intake) and top (exhaust).

Phase C: The Cooling Tunnel
- Dig: A 10ft long trench, 1ft deep.
- Pipe: Lay a 4-inch perforated PVC pipe (or cast a small section of Aerocement pipe).
- Wicking: Connect a water reservoir to feed the pipe via capillary action.
- Connection: Connect the Exhaust of the chimney to the Intake of the tunnel.

Phase D: Testing & Data Collection
Tools Needed:
- 2x Thermocouples (digital thermometer probes).
- Anemometer (to measure wind speed).
- Pyranometer (optional, to measure sun intensity).

Success Criteria:
- Exhaust Temp > 180F.
- Tunnel Output < 60F.
- Measurable airflow without a fan.
